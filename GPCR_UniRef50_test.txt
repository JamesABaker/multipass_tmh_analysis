CC   -!- FUNCTION: Receptor for apelin receptor early endogenous ligand
CC       (apela) and apelin (apln) hormones coupled to G proteins that
CC       inhibit adenylate cyclase activity (PubMed:17336905,
CC       PubMed:17336906, PubMed:24316148, PubMed:24407481). Plays a key
CC       role in early development such as gastrulation and heart
CC       morphogenesis by acting as a receptor for apela hormone, promoting
CC       endoderm and mesendoderm cell migration and regulating the
CC       migration of cells fated to become myocardial progenitors,
CC       respectively (PubMed:17336905, PubMed:17336906, PubMed:24316148,
CC       PubMed:24407481). Plays a role in various processes in adults such
CC       as regulation of blood vessel formation, blood pressure, heart
CC       contractility, and heart failure (By similarity). Acts redundantly
CC       with agtrl1a in heart development (PubMed:17336906).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P35414, ECO:0000269|PubMed:17336905,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:17336906, ECO:0000269|PubMed:24316148,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:24407481}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell membrane
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P79960}; Multi-pass membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P79960}. Note=Internalized to the cytoplasm
CC       after exposure to apelin (apln). After exposure to apelin receptor
CC       early endogenous ligand (apela), internalized from the cell
CC       surface into an endosomal recycling compartment, from where it is
CC       recycled to the cell membrane. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P35414,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P79960}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Mesendodermal expression at the blastoderm
CC       margin appears by 4.5 hpf. At early gastrulation, expression is
CC       maintained ventrolaterally while expression in dorsal cells and
CC       random deep cells declines. During gastrulation and segmentation,
CC       expression is maintained in adaxial, intermediate, and lateral
CC       plate mesoderm. During late segmentation, expressed in several
CC       regions including the forming heart. By 24 hpf, expressed in the
CC       dorsal aorta, caudal vein, and intersomitic blood vessels.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:17336905}.

CC   -!- FUNCTION: Receptor for R-spondins that potentiates the canonical
CC       Wnt signaling pathway and is involved in the formation of various
CC       organs. Upon binding to R-spondins (RSPO1, RSPO2, RSPO3 or RSPO4),
CC       associates with phosphorylated LRP6 and frizzled receptors that
CC       are activated by extracellular Wnt receptors, triggering the
CC       canonical Wnt signaling pathway to increase expression of target
CC       genes. In contrast to classical G-protein coupled receptors, does
CC       not activate heterotrimeric G-proteins to transduce the signal.
CC       Its function as activator of the Wnt signaling pathway is required
CC       for the development of various organs, including liver, kidney,
CC       intestine, bone, reproductive tract and eye. May also act as a
CC       receptor for norrin (NDP), such results however require additional
CC       confirmation in vivo. Required during spermatogenesis to activate
CC       the Wnt signaling pathway in peritubular myoid cells. Required for
CC       the maintenance of intestinal stem cells and Paneth cell
CC       differentiation in postnatal intestinal crypts. Acts as a
CC       regulator of bone formation and remodeling. Involved in kidney
CC       development; required for maintaining the ureteric bud in an
CC       undifferentiated state. Involved in the development of the
CC       anterior segment of the eye. Required during erythropoiesis. Also
CC       acts as a negative regulator of innate immunity by inhibiting
CC       TLR2/TLR4 associated pattern-recognition and proinflammatory
CC       cytokine production. Plays an important role in regulating the
CC       circadian rhythms of plasma lipids, partially through regulating
CC       the rhythmic expression of MTTP (PubMed:24353284).

CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Strong variations in phenotypes, probably
CC       depending on the strain background used in the different
CC       experiments. According to a first report done in a C57BL/6
CC       background, mice show intra-uterine growth retardation leading to
CC       embryonic and perinatal lethality (PubMed:15192078). A lethal
CC       phenotype was confirmed by other groups. In some cases however,
CC       few mice survive until adulthood. Mice that survive show wide
CC       spectrum of anterior segment dysgenesis phenotypes, including
CC       microphthalmia, iris hypoplasia, irdiocorneal angle malformation,
CC       cornea dysgenesis and cataract (PubMed:18424556). Defects in
CC       osteoblast differentiation and mineralization during embryonic
CC       bone formation are also observed. Postnatal mice that survive show
CC       defects in bone remodeling (PubMed:19605502). According to other
CC       reports done in other strain backgrounds, mice do not die and
CC       display defects in the male reproductive tract during the
CC       postnatal period (PubMed:16406039, PubMed:17079737,
CC       PubMed:23533175). Mice also show small kidneys, in which the total
CC       number and density of the glomeruli are decreased
CC       (PubMed:16785743). The use of conditional knockouts, leads to
CC       defects in hair placode formation, possibly due to reduced
CC       keratinocyte migration (PubMed:17079737, PubMed:17850793).
CC       Decrease in epithelial cell proliferation and strong reduction in
CC       terminal differentiation of Paneth cells in postnatal intestinal
CC       crypts (PubMed:21508962, PubMed:23393138). Fetuses display
CC       transient anemia during midgestation and abnormal definitive
CC       erythropoiesis (PubMed:18955481). Mice are also more susceptible
CC       and have much higher mortality to lipopolysaccharide (LPS)
CC       stimulation due to over-activated innate immune response
CC       (PubMed:23589304). Conditional knockout of both Lgr4 and Lgr5 in
CC       the gut results in Wnt signaling inhibition and results in the
CC       rapid demise of intestinal crypts (PubMed:21727895).
